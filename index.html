Why are you traveling alone?

The first question any prospective solo-traveler must ask, before taking any other steps, is to ask why? This may seem like an absurd first step, and for some, even many, the answer is obvious. Nevertheless, it fundamentally changes the outlook one has in regards to long-distance travel. Below are some common reasons:

  • Just getting away from it all?
  • A personal challenge?
  • Self-reflection or time with your thoughts?
  • Simply no other choice?

Getting ahead of the headache

With your reasoning in mind, it's time to anticipate the difficulties you could run into on such a journey. Don't start worrying about what to bring with you, where you're going, and more logistical details just yet; instead, plan ahead for the unforeseen. Here's a few examples:

  • If going by car; running low on gas, damaged tires, road closures and construction.
  • If going by bus; cancellations, tight transfer windows, and outdated timetable information.
  • If going by foot; hazardous weather, long stretches without shelter, damage to shoes or other equipment.
